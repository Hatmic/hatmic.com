A middle school student from China who is interested in both music and computer science fields.

Project

When I was in elementary school, I was introduced to Python programming, but at the time, my interest wasn’t deep, and the curriculum seemed quite complex to me. I only learned the basic syntax. Later, I took more Python courses and studied for two years before I stopped. I felt cheated by the training institution because their teaching was too slow and too basic.

Under Hank’s recommendation, I discovered Codemao and used visual programming to create many works during fifth and sixth grade. I gained some recognition on the Codemao website, made many friends, and some of them have been very helpful to me. We still maintain our friendship to this day.

In seventh grade, I was introduced to informatics competitions and used C++. At first, I wasn’t interested, but I gradually grew to like it. However, in the end, I gave it up. I learned some algorithms and data structures, but the knowledge I gained was only useful for solving problems, which I didn’t enjoy.

Encouraged by my friends Coppist and Larmoo, I started learning front-end web development. I taught myself using tutorials I found on Bilibili. To this day, I’ve created many simple projects, such as blog.hatmic.com and pro.hatmic.com, which you can visit (although they are quite rudimentary, and I plan to discontinue them). I am currently working on the content for hatmic.com, which I find very enjoyable as it allows me to be creative and make something of my own.
